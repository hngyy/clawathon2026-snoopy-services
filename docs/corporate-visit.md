# What Is a Corporate Visit?

A **Corporate Visit** at VNG is a strategic touchpoint — not just a tour. It shapes how VNG is
perceived by partners, stakeholders, and the broader ecosystem. Each visit aims to:

- **Introduce capabilities** — VNG's strengths across business, technology, and ecosystem.
- **Build relationships** — meaningful connections with partners and stakeholders.
- **Create foundations** — a basis for future collaboration.

Corporate Visits are coordinated end-to-end by the **Corporate Communications (CC) team**.
In this bot, the CC coordinator is the **owner** role; the internal employee organizing a visit
is the **requester** role.

## Structure: Tour + Meeting

A Corporate Visit has two components — a visit may be **tour only** or **tour + meeting**.

1. **Tour (Campus & Data Center)** — showcase the working environment, scale, culture, and
   technical infrastructure; integrate insights from relevant Business Units.
   *Goal: build trust through real exposure.*
2. **Meeting** *(optional)* — align on business needs, share expertise and direction, explore
   concrete collaboration.
   *Goal: turn interest into discussion and potential action.*

## Core Objectives — the 3Ps

- **Perception** — guests understand who VNG is today: not only games, but a growing technology
  ecosystem.
- **Positioning** — VNG seen as a credible, capable partner; the Campus as a model of innovation
  (AI, fintech, infrastructure).
- **Possibility** — the visit leads to next steps: follow-up meetings, partnership discussions,
  media or strategic engagement.

> Tour builds trust. Meeting drives opportunities.

## Flow Overview

Every visit runs across three phases, coordinated by CC for a seamless, high-impact experience.

| Phase | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Before** | Preparation & alignment |
| **During** | Execution & experience delivery |
| **After** | Follow-up & leverage |

### Before — Intake & Mapping

Requests arrive via internal channels (SMT / MT / EA / BU partners) or direct external contact
(email / phone). CC then:

- Understands the **guest profile and background**.
- Identifies the **purpose** of the visit.
- Defines the **priority level (tier)**.
- Maps relevant **Business Units** and proposes the structure: **tour only** or **tour + meeting**.

### Before — Logistics

Prepared in advance to ensure a professional, seamless experience:

- **Schedule & rooms** — schedule alignment and meeting-room booking.
- **Tea break & refreshments** — tea break, water, refreshments for guests.
- **Welcome setup** — welcome screen and guest greeting.
- **Partner gifts** — gifts prepared for visiting partners.
- **Technical setup** — laptop, pointer, presentation materials.
- **Media & recording** — photo/video support and audio recording if required.

### Before — Speakers (for meetings)

Select speakers by **relevant expertise** and **appropriate seniority** (matched to the guests).
Align beforehand on key messages, presentation flow, talking points, and expected questions.

### During — Execution & Experience Delivery

1. **Reception** — welcome guests at the lobby; brief the agenda and flow.
2. **Tour (core experience)** — conduct the Campus and/or Data Center tour; share stories about
   culture, business, and technology.
3. **Meeting (if applicable)** — run presentations and discussions; manage agenda and timing.

**CC role:** coordinate the entire flow, ensure message consistency, handle on-site issues.

### After — Follow-up & Content Leverage

- **Content processing** — transcribe key discussions if needed; select and organize photos.
- **Internal leverage** — share materials with PR and EB teams; amplify via media and internal
  channels.
- **External follow-up** — send visit recap and retouched photos; provide materials; collect
  feedback from guests.
